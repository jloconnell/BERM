Microspatial Variation in Tidal marsh Soil Temperature

We used field data and Landsat 8 land surface temperature to demonstrate that small elevation differences were negatively correlated with significant soil temperature variation on the marsh platform, irrespective of tidal flooding. Biological reactions are temperature-dependent, and these findings indicate that metabolic soil processes will vary over short distances in tidal marshes. This is important for accurately estimating marsh metabolism and predicting how changes in temperature will affect future productivity.

The PhenoCam Smart Classifier

We created the smart classier to identify optimal digital images for phenology analysis in tidal salt marshes. The smart classifier is a within image pixel classification algorithm that accounts for image context. It can be used to filter PhenoCam imagery or to monitor changes in environmental conditions.
